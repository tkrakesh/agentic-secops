# PB-024: Insider Threat Investigation

## Playbook Metadata
- **Playbook ID**: PB-024
- **Version**: 1.8
- **Last Updated**: 2026-01-05
- **Owner**: Insider Threat Programme (ITP) / HR Security
- **SLA**: Evidence preserved within 4 hours; no disruptive action without Legal approval
- **Approval Required**: CISO + General Counsel (mandatory dual approval)
- **Classification**: RESTRICTED — SOC Director and above only

## Trigger Conditions
1. Data staging behaviour detected (bulk file copy, archive creation before off-boarding)
2. USB mass storage OR cloud sync exfiltration indicators (OneDrive/Dropbox large uploads)
3. Off-hours access to sensitive systems outside normal business hours pattern
4. HR-flagged user (resignation submitted, under investigation, or disciplinary action active)

**MITRE ATT&CK Coverage**: T1052.001 (Exfil over USB), T1567 (Exfil to Cloud), T1530 (Data from Cloud Storage), T1078 (Valid Accounts abuse)

## ⚠ LEGAL NOTE
This playbook involves covert monitoring. **DO NOT** alert the subject. Any actions taken must be documented and reviewed by Legal before being used in disciplinary or law enforcement proceedings.

## Action Sequence

### Step 1 — Silent Monitoring Mode (Covert — Zero Alert to User)
- Increase SIEM collection verbosity for all activity by the identified user (to DEBUG level)
- Enable extended EDR telemetry: file access, USB events, print jobs, screen capture if policy allows
- Do NOT trigger any user-visible alerts or notifications
- **Expected Duration**: 5 minutes

### Step 2 — Preserve Existing Evidence (Before Access Changes)
- Immediately snapshot current state of user's workstation (virtual) or request physical image
- Pull print logs, email archives, USB event logs for past 90 days
- Export all DLP events involving the user from past 90 days
- Archive email inbox to legal hold (e-Discovery platform)
- Hash all evidence immediately upon collection
- **Expected Duration**: 30 minutes

### Step 3 — Notify Legal, HR, and CIRO
- Brief General Counsel and CHRO jointly — do not brief independently
- Provide preliminary evidence summary (not conclusions — let Legal assess)
- Obtain formal authorisation for continued monitoring duration
- **Expected Duration**: Immediate — parallel to evidence collection

### Step 4 — Restrict Access Incrementally (If Approved by Legal)
- Remove access to highest-risk systems first (data warehouses, source code, customer data)
- Keep email and core productivity access active to avoid alerting subject
- Document every access change with Legal reference number
- **Expected Duration**: Per Legal directive

### Step 5 — Prepare CIRT Brief
- Compile chronological evidence timeline
- Draft incident summary for CIRO review
- Assess regulatory reporting requirements (if customer data was accessed/exfiltrated)
- **Expected Duration**: 2 hours

## Estimated Investigation Time
**72 hours** for initial evidence review. Full investigation: 1–4 weeks (Legal-driven).

## Post-Investigation Actions
- Coordinate with HR for employment action (if confirmed)
- Law enforcement involvement assessment with Legal
- System access review across entire peer group (insider threat usually not isolated)
- Policy review: DLP controls, USB policy, data classification enforcement

## Exclusion Criteria
- False positive: bulk file copy for approved project delivery
- NEVER activate without Legal authorisation — wrongful monitoring liability is significant
